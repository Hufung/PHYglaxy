This page covers key concepts such as motion, forces, work, energy, and power, providing explanations, formulas, examples, and practice questions to help you prepare for your exams.

Position, Distance, and Motion

Key Concepts

  • Position: Location relative to a reference point.
  • Distance: Total path length traveled (scalar).
  • Displacement: Shortest path between start and end points (vector).
  • Speed: Distance per unit time (scalar, e.g., \( v = \frac{d}{t} \)).
  • Velocity: Displacement per unit time (vector, e.g., \( v = \frac{s}{t} \)).
  • Acceleration: Rate of change of velocity (e.g., \( a = \frac{\Delta v}{\Delta t} \)).

Equations of Motion

For uniformly accelerated motion:

  • \( v = u + at \)
  • \( s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2 \)
  • \( v^2 = u^2 + 2as \)

Where: \( u \) = initial velocity, \( v \) = final velocity, \( a \) = acceleration, \( t \) = time, \( s \) = displacement.

Example

A car starts from rest (\( u = 0 \)) and accelerates at \( 2 \, \text{m/s}^2 \) for 5 seconds. What is its final velocity?

Using \( v = u + at \): \( v = 0 + 2 \times 5 = 10 \, \text{m/s} \).

Practice Questions

  1. A ball is dropped from a height with \( u = 0 \) and \( a = 9.81 \, \text{m/s}^2 \). How far does it fall in 2 seconds?
  2. A cyclist travels 50 m in 10 s. What is their average speed?

Newton’s Laws of Motion

First Law: Law of Inertia

An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion with the same speed and direction unless acted upon by an unbalanced force.

A book on a table stays at rest because the downward gravitational force is balanced by the upward normal force.

Second Law: Law of Acceleration

The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass. Formula: \( F = ma \)

A 2 kg object accelerates at \( 5 \, \text{m/s}^2 \). The force is \( F = 2 \times 5 = 10 \, \text{N} \).

Third Law: Law of Action and Reaction

For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

When you jump off a boat, you push the boat backward, and it pushes you forward.

Key Formulas

  • \( F = ma \)
  • \( W = mg \) (Weight, where \( g = 9.81 \, \text{m/s}^2 \))

Practice Questions

  1. A car of mass 1000 kg accelerates from rest to 20 m/s in 10 s. What is the net force?
  2. Two objects, A (2 kg) and B (3 kg), experience the same force. Which has greater acceleration?

Work, Energy, and Power

Work

Work is the transfer of energy when a force moves an object. Formula: \( W = Fd \cos\theta \) (where \( \theta \) is the angle between force and displacement).

A 10 N force moves an object 5 m in the same direction (\( \theta = 0^\circ \)). Work = \( 10 \times 5 \times \cos 0 = 50 \, \text{J} \).

Energy

Kinetic Energy (K.E.): Energy of motion. Formula: \( K.E. = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 \)

Gravitational Potential Energy (P.E.): Energy due to height. Formula: \( P.E. = mgh \)

A 1 kg object moving at 4 m/s has \( K.E. = \frac{1}{2} \times 1 \times 4^2 = 8 \, \text{J} \).

Conservation of Energy

Total mechanical energy (K.E. + P.E.) is conserved if no external forces (like friction) do work.

Power

Rate of energy transfer. Formula: \( P = \frac{W}{t} \) or \( P = Fv \)

A machine does 100 J of work in 5 s. Power = \( \frac{100}{5} = 20 \, \text{W} \).

Practice Questions

  1. A 2 kg object is lifted 3 m. What is its potential energy (\( g = 9.81 \, \text{m/s}^2 \))?
  2. A 500 N force moves an object 10 m in 2 s. What is the power?
